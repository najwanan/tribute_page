Before Fame

Monroe was born Norma Jeane Mortenson at the Los Angeles County Hospital in Los Angeles, California, on June 1, 1926. In the summer of 1933, Norma Jean and her mom Gladys bought a small house in Hollywood A year later, Gladys had a mental breakdown and was diagnosed with paranoid schizophrenia. Young Marilyn would spend the next few years of her life as a ward of the state, until her first marriage at 16 to the boy next door, a Marine.

The Start of the End

As she became the new Hollywood It Girl, Marilyn learned that the industry was an unkind place for women. A frequent target of sexism, double standards, mental abuse, resulted in anxiety and chronic insomnia. She turned to drugs for cope. Barbiturates, amphetamines, and alcohol, which also exacerbated her problems on set. It started a destructive cycle in which any assertion of control Marilyn wanted over her career was considered "difficult behavior", she would use to cope, rinse, repeat. She would become severely addicted around 1956.
